Why Donate Blood?

The Gift of Life

Blood donation is a simple, safe procedure that takes less than an hour but can make a lifetime of difference for patients in need.

Every 2 seconds someone in the country needs blood. Trauma patients, surgery patients, transplant recipients, and those being treated for diseases like cancer all need blood.

  • Safe and easy process
  • No cost to donate
  • Regular check-ups included

Frequently Asked Questions

Most people can donate blood if they are in good health, weigh at least 50 kg (110 lbs), are between 18-65 years old (varies by country), and meet certain medical eligibility criteria. There are some temporary and permanent deferrals based on health conditions, medications, and travel history.

The minimum interval between whole blood donations is 56 days for men and 84 days for women. For platelet donation, you can donate every 7 days, up to 24 times a year.

Yes, donating blood is very safe. All equipment is sterile, used only once, and then discarded. The actual blood donation takes about 8-10 minutes, and the entire process from registration to recovery takes about an hour.

Before donating blood, get a good night's sleep, eat a healthy meal, and drink plenty of fluids. Avoid fatty foods before donation. Bring your ID and a list of any medications you're taking.

Donated blood can be separated into its components (red cells, platelets, and plasma) and used to help multiple patients. Blood is used in surgeries, trauma cases, cancer treatment, chronic illnesses, and other medical conditions requiring blood transfusions.
